FRANKIE, HOW DO I START LISTENING TO GHOST?

What a great question! I just may be the perfect person to tell you where to start, because I've been a fan of this band for years. Literally!

In fact, to the right, you'll find a compilation of most of my Ghost fanart from age 15 to 18 (2022-2025). Hopefully that proves that I know what I'm talking about.

The first thing you have to understand when exploring Ghost is that their band is essentially a great big gimmick. They started out as a masked anonymous band, with the lead singer going by the pseudonym Papa Emeritus I.

The second thing you have to understand when exploring Ghost is that however heavy you're thinking they're going to sound based on their looks, divide that by 50. Ghost is not a heavy band.

...Well, for the most part, at least. What I mean by this is that, despite their heavy metal sound, Ghost features largely clean vocals and melodic tunes. Some practiced metalheads have spoken about how they saw the visuals of the band (the makeup, the album covers, etc) and thought it was going to sound a lot different than what they got.

For some, this is a good thing. For others, it's the bane of their existence.

Basically, if I had to compare Ghost to a band that most people know, it's KISS. They're a Satanic KISS. If that sounds like it's gonna be your thing, then keep reading!

THE MUSIC

To be honest, you've probably already heard Ghost without even realizing it. Their song, Mary On A Cross, went crazy viral on TikTok in the summer of 2022. Cirice was a similarly popular song, though not to the level of MOAC.

It's very important to note that MOAC is very, very different sonically when compared to most of Ghost's music. It's from the EP Seven Inches of Satanic Panic, which is canonically from 1969, so each of the songs from that album have more of a 60s vibe.

You might be completely thrown off by that statement, considering the EP was released in 2019. I'll explain later. For now, let's keep our eyes on the prize: the music.

I always start by recommending Meliora to those who have never properly listened to Ghost before. It's pretty much right in the middle of their discography, which offers a great look into both the past and future of their sound.
Upon listening, if you prefer the darker, more eclectic sound of Meliora, then you can go backwards and listen to Opus and Infestissumam. If you prefer the cleaner, more organized sound of Meliora, then you can go forwards and listen to Prequelle, Impera, and Skeletá.

Opus, being their first album, is understandably the roughest. That being said, it still holds up! It's spooky, it's overtly Satanic to a borderline humorous degree, and it's a relatively short listen (34 minutes).

Infestissumam might be their strangest album. It's a little all over the place, but has some really heavy hitters, like Year Zero and Monstrance Clock, which are still played at concerts to this day.

I believe Meliora to be their best album (which is part of why I recommend it, I won't lie). It's consistent yet unique. Go listen to it already!

Prequelle is what I'd call their poppiest album, with hits like Dance Macabre and Pro Memoria bringing a lighter tune to Ghost's discography. That being said, the album is actually about death, so... it's not as poppy as it looks.

Impera is similar to Meliora in that it is very consistent in sound and lyric quality. It's largely adored by both critics and fans, so it's not a bad place to start, either. I'm just annoying and find too much consistency to be boring, so it's not my favorite. Twenties and Respite On The Spitalfields are great, though!

Finally, Skeletá is kind of like if Prequelle and Infestissumam had a baby. Prequelle in sound quality, Infestissumam in its eclectic nature. You either love it or you hate it.

I hope this explanation of the albums helps you in your journey through Ghost's discography! I didn't even delve into the cover EPs, but they're definitely something to check out as well. Popestar has some of my favorite covers they've made.

I feel like I'm forgetting something, though...

THE LORE

Oh yeah, there's lore.

So, you remember how I said this band is a gimmick band? The brains and vocals of the band, Tobias Forge, basically came up with this character to play rather than perform on stage as himself. That's Papa Emeritus, who I mentioned before.

Here's an excerpt of what their MySpace page said way back in 2010 that explains the concept of the band better than I could:

"Ghost is the name of a devil worshipping ministry that – in order to spread their unholy gospels and, furthermore, trick mankind into believing that the end is ultimately a good thing – have decided to use the ever so popular rock music medium as a way to achieve their ends.
Standing motionless and anonymous beneath the painted faces, hoods and robes which their sect demand, the six nameless ghouls of Ghost deliver litanies of sexually pulsating heavy rock music and romantic lyrics, which glorify and glamorise the disgusting and sacrilegious, with the simple intention to communicate a message of pure evil via the most effective device they can find: entertainment."

Basically they're like a Satanic gospel band. Cool, right?

Essentially, with each new album comes a "new" frontman. I use quotation marks because they're all Tobias Forge wearing different masks.
Opus Eponymous began with Papa Emeritus I, who passed the role down to Papa Emeritus II when Infestissumam came, who then passed the role to Papa Emeritus III with the release of Meliora...

...and then Papa Emeritus III got dragged off stage and that's when the Chapters (which you can find on YouTube) started establishing a proper storyline. We're introduced to the characters of Cardinal Copia (played by Forge), Sister Imperator, and Papa Nihil.

Do you remember how I said Seven Inches of Satanic Panic was "released in 1969"? That's because the "Ghost project" actually started in the 60s with Papa Nihil as the frontman. In reality, I'm sure Forge just wanted to toy with the sound of 60s rock, but it's a fun little explanation for the abrupt change in sound.

Anyways, I won't get into the lore too much because that's not the point of this website. I only mention it because it is a large part of why the Internet likes this band so much. You don't need to follow the lore to enjoy the music - they aren't truly connected whatsoever. However, it is fun... so I encourage you to watch the Chapters if you're intrigued!
