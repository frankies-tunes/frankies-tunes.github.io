FRANKIE, HOW DO I START LISTENING TO OINGO BOINGO?

Well, the most common way is to go on Spotify, search up "Oingo Boingo", and click play... but I'll give you a better way.

If you like new wave music, you'll probably like Oingo Boingo. Commonly described as Devo's underrated cousin, Oingo Boingo is (in my opinion) one of the best new wave acts out there.

It's a real shame they didn't take off, because their work is great! Danny Elfman has a really emotive voice that fits the new wave/ska/rock energy the band puts out. At the same time, I'm kind of glad they didn't take off, because we might not have some of the best scores in film if Elfman stuck with Oingo Boingo.

That's right: while you might not have heard his work in Oingo Boingo, you've probably heard his film scores. If you've watched The Simpsons in any capacity, you've probably heard the intro - composed by Elfman. He also did a lot of work with Tim Burton, which is what I primarily knew him for before getting into this band.

The best way I can describe Oingo Boingo is a strange, absurd conglomerate of synth, brass, and xylophones, mixed with some great wit and satire. If that interests you, why not give them a listen?

THE MUSIC

While Oingo Boingo is certainly the most niche band featured on this site, they still have some presence in pop culture. Did you know they're featured in Stranger Things, just like Kate Bush? Their song Just Another Day plays in Season 2. Dead Man's Party is in Buffy the Vampire Slayer, and Weird Science was made for a movie with the same name!

I've been a pretty big Boingo fan for the last five years. I discovered them when I was 14 thanks to my friends on the Internet, who were all equally into strange, niche bands that are only popular online.

It's given me a great appreciation for satire and counterculture as a whole. So, honestly... I'd advise you start at the very beginning of their discography.

This might be shocking, considering a lot of people typically stay away from an artist's first album until they're more acquainted with their work. Normally, I'd do the same. But Oingo Boingo's first album, Only A Lad, isn't just a great album. It also sets the tone for the majority of their discography.

So that's my advice. Start at the beginning, and listen to their albums chronologically. Oingo Boingo is that simple.

Only A Lad is an album with all bangers packed with societal and political commentary - some of which may shock and/or beguile you. It opens with the track Little Girls... stay with me here, alright!?
This song should tell you how this album is going to go: they commentate on societal issues by singing from the perspective of evil, no-good people (that hold beliefs they do not hold). That's the point of a lot of the songs on this album, including but not limited to Capitalism and Perfect System.

Nothing To Fear is my favorite Oingo Boingo album, which is quite similar to Only A Lad in the commentary and sound - though it's decidedly less shocking. Grey Matter and Private Life are two of my all-time favorite Oingo Boingo tracks.

Good For Your Soul continues Oingo Boingo's biting sociopolitical commentary with tracks such as Wake Up (It's 1984), which is about... you guessed it, 1984 by George Orwell. Similarly, Little Guns is about war and propaganda!

Dead Man's Party marks the point where the band focuses a little less on commentary and a little more on making great music. I've already mentioned three songs from this album, so I won't linger on it much longer, but it's a pretty solid piece of work and a fun record to put on during October.

Honestly, I'm not really sure how to describe Boi-Ngo. It's good. Great, even. It's just not something I can describe in just a few words. It's very 80s! Home Again and Outrageous are my favorite tracks on the album.

Dark At The End Of The Tunnel is an album I didn't listen to until last year, which is something I regret a lot. It's really good! It's very different compared to the sound of the rest of their work, but there's something about it I just really enjoy. It's dark and gloomy but with a Boingo twist. Right To Know and Dream Somehow are great tracks with heaps of xylophone, which I love.

I hope this explanation of the albums helps you in your journey through Oingo Boingo's discography! Pretty soon, you'll be compelled to listen to Devo, XTC, and... gasp, maybe even Talking Heads. New wave is an illness that spreads fast, my friend. Be wary.
