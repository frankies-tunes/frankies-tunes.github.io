FRANKIE, HOW DO I START LISTENING TO KATE BUSH?

It's easier than you'd think.

Kate Bush is an incredibly eclectic artist with a wide variety of albums to choose from. That means there's probably going to be something that appeals to you within her work, if you enjoy 80s music from the English.

She's also been pretty darn popular ever since her feature in Stranger Things with her song Running Up That Hill, which you can find the music video for to the right. Pretty much everyone in the world knows this song now, which is awesome!

Personally, a big part of why I love her music is her lyricism. The things she chooses to write about, and the way she chooses to write about them is very poetic. If you like to spend time analyzing lyrics, there's a lot for you to analyze within her discography.

However, her lyricism is not the only appeal. She utilizes sound in a way that makes your head spin a little bit at first, before you realize none of it is disjointed. It all works really well together and is only emphasized by her unique voice.

THE MUSIC

Again, I'd argue you've probably heard her music before. Prior to the explosion of popularity Running Up That Hill recieved, her most popular song was Wuthering Heights, written about the book of the same name.

Similar to Ghost, Army Dreamers is a song that became popular on TikTok for a short while in 2024. If I remember correctly, Babooshka had a brief stint on TikTok as well!

For me, I discovered Kate through the album Hounds Of Love, so I'll always suggest new listeners start there. It's her most mainstream album with a more palatable sound, but it doesn't stop you from getting a look into her more unique style. It's also a concept album (excluding the first five songs), so if you're into that, then you'll probably enjoy following along with the disturbing story of The Ninth Wave!

If you want to know more, here's an article from the BBC that gives a few fun facts about the album. I would suggest listening to the album first, but either way works.

While Kate's discography isn't as simple as Ghost's, there are a couple ways you can go after listening to Hounds Of Love.
If you like the poppier, mainstream sound, I'd listen to The Sensual World and The Red Shoes. If you like the absurd, artsy elements to the album, I'd listen to Never for Ever and The Dreaming. If you want something completely different, I'd go for The Kick Inside and Lionheart, which have a very prominent 70s sound.

Now, I'm not an expert on Kate's entire discography, unfortunately. However, other people are! So here's a link to a diagram that offers a better journey through her work than I can provide.

The Kick Inside is very close to being my favorite Kate Bush album. The late 70s rock vibe is perfectly acquainted with her beautiful vocals, especially in songs like Feel It and L'Amour Looks Something Like You.

Lionheart is very similar to The Kick Inside sonically, which makes sense, because they came out in the same year! There's a little more experimentation with this album though, which can be heard when we listen to songs like Hammer Horror and Wow.

Never for Ever continues the trek into experimental sound, with Babooshka utilizing the sound of breaking glass throughout the track, as well as her vocals getting particularly emotive in The Wedding List.

The Dreaming is my favorite Kate Bush album, and probably the most experimental part of her discography (that I've heard thus far). Sat In Your Lap has intense drums and fluctuating time signatures while Kate's own vocals fluctuate between yelling, grunting, and crooning. Meanwhile, Get Out Of My House is genuinely kind of scary. I love it.

Obviously, we know Hounds Of Love. I won't linger on it for too long, but some of my favorite underrated tracks on this album are The Big Sky and Hello Earth.

The Sensual World is another really solid album, one that definitely sits in my top three. It's a very emotional, human pop album. I particularly enjoy Deeper Understanding and Between A Man And A Woman.

Finally, The Red Shoes is a romp, similar to The Sensual World in its humanity. Eat The Music and Rubberband Girl always manage to get me dancing on even the worst of days.

I hope this explanation of the albums helps you in your journey through Kate Bush's discography! There's a lot I have to learn still, but I can only hope I know enough to encourage others to enjoy her music as much as I do.
